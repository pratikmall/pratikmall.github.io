    KINDLE AUTHOR SERVICES

    Team: I was the lead designer. Worked with my design internship mentor, a product manager, my design manager, design director.














    About Kindle Ecosystem

    Amazon's Kindle Ecosystem provides authors(both independant and managed) publish eBooks and paperbacks for free and reach millions of readers on Amazon.






    PROBLEM

    “

    As an independant author I have trouble finding the right individuals to help me editing, design and marketing for my book.
    - Groupon User


    ”

    Problem Highlights

    • An independant author has no one-stop shop for publishing services that they need after writing their manuscript.

    • The current tools avaialble to hire individuals aren't focussed on book publishing

    • It takes a lot of time to vet individuals you want to hire and often there is expectation mismatch on what to expect







    HOW MIGHT WE

    How might we help independant author hire vetted individuals to help with editing, design and marketing services for their book?






    KINDLE AUTHOR SERVICES VISION

    We designed the vision of a new offering from Amazon called Kindle Author Services. Due to the short duration of my internship, I focussed on the purchase experience for editing services.


    Educating Users

    During user testing, we found that some users weren’t sure of how this new service worked which prompted us to add a ‘how it works’ section on the hero. This helped educate them about the service and helped build trust in the system right at the outset.



    Show pricing upfront for editing

    One of the key design decisions we made was to show price upfront by having authors enter the number of words in their manuscript. On the service provider side, this is a typical way they estimated the workload for a client and accordingly charge them. We decided to center the editing services pricing model around this logic. This made sure that the expectation is set right and back and forth is reduced to get quotes from service providers.

    CLEAR Visual Hierarchy

    Not all independent authors were well versed with the notion of editing and what it entails. Hence, it was important to lay out different editing services with a clear visual representation of what to expect from each service. Having a clear visual hierarchy of what a particular service entails helped us educate authors.

    Persistent Context

    It was important for users to know where they are in the purchase process of a service. Hence adding persistent context throughout the purchase funnel was important.

    SHOWING Provider information

    There are multiple factors that go into the decision making of selecting the right provider for an editing service. Hence, providing authors with the right information about providers was important. Authors could also filter providers based on those criteria.





    RESULTS AND LEARNINGS

    RESULTS

    At the end of my internship, we had a full-proof user-tested vision for Kindle author Services. I was pretty happy with what I was able to accomplish at the end of those 10 weeks. The vision was to be brought to yearly roadmap later that year after my internship and another full-time designer would lead the charge on the same.

    LEARNINGS

    Stakeholder Challenges: A week before my internship start date, the full time PM for my project decided to move on to another role. So, it was challenging to work on the project without a PM for the first 3 weeks. I had to make sure I kept the momentum going until a new PM was assigned. With the help of my mentor, I was able to narrow down and scope the project to quite a bit before the new PM entered.

    Adopting a Design System: This was the first time I worked with a full-proof design system that was recently created and was yet to be adopted by the entire product suite. Hence, understanding the detailed nuances from the design system designer and using the design system components correctly was quite a learning experience in itself.

    PROCESS



    Past Research
    The first step in the process was to learn from past user research done by the team to gather institutional knowledge and empathize with the problem.

    Inspiration Gathering
    We did an extensive competitive inspiration gathering to see what other players are doing in this space. We gathered a bunch of ideas which gave us a head start into the design process.

    Iterative Design
    As I started designing, I went through multiple rounds of feedback from multiple stakeholders to make design decisions. This helped us arrive at a design that we felt was good enough to be user-tested to validate the effectiveness of the vision.

    Guerilla User Testing
    Next, I learned a new user testing method(Guerilla User Testing) to test my designs within the short time frame I had. This helped us gather valuable insights that helped us make some key design decisions.

    Final Design and Handoff
    I went through a couple more round of iterations based on the feedback from the user testing. I then handed off the final designs to the designer who would continue to extend the work I did after the end of my internship.
